Cyber Security

most of the time when I interact with many people one of the questions, they normally ask is how can they be safe from cyber attacks? well in as much as I hate to say this, when it comes to cyber security safety is never guaranteed

As they normally say there is no system which is secure and if it is secure then its only safe because it is less popular and therefore it hasn't attracted the attention of many hackers .A less popular systems doesn't attract many hackers simply because they have no value or their value isn’t worth what the hacker wanted. Even in real life you only go after things that are worth the time and resources that you will spend

So what do we do?

In as much as our data or systems can’t be safe from hackers what we can do is minimize the risk in which this data or systems can be exposed to this hacker

Cyber security is like a disaster that you can’t prevent it from happening but you can minimize the damage it causes in case it occurs. This can be applied by using the prevention measures that are available today i.e., ensuring that your data is encrypted before transmitting it over the internet. Take this example when you are building a house, you will have to ensure that this house is secure. The question is how do you do that? well the first thing you do is to ensure that the compound is fenced and it has a gate or gates. That way you will have minimized the entry points to the compound and it doesn’t rule out the fact that someone clever cannot climb over the fence and get into the compound but at least it is better than when it had no fence. secondly, you ensure that at the door there is a grill at the door if you are in a hostile environment, then after the grill there is the door. The gate, the grills at the door and the door itself act as layers of security to this house. that means if you want to get into the house you will have to go through the gate, the grills and the door before you finally get into the house. This doesn’t rule out the fact that someone can jump over the fence and get into the house through the window but it will require some extra effort

Another thing that you have to keep in mind is how strong are this layer of security.it is easier to get through a wooden door compared to a metallic door. We all use passwords in our day-to-day activities i.e., log into our devices or the various accounts i.e., emails. Simple predictable passwords are easy to crack compared to strong passwords. Well, someone may argue that passwords are always encrypted so they are safe. The fact that they are encrypted means that they can be decryption example when setting up a Wi-Fi password most people use the number 1 to 8 just because the password should be at least be 8 characters. That is so easy to predict if I’m a hacker. Another mistake that people make is that people make especially when setting up passwords for online accounts i.e., emails accounts is using the first letter of their first name and the first letter of their last name and then their number. That pattern can be be easily predicted by hackers.
